Historical Roots

Phuket's prosperity was built on its rich natural resources. Long before it became a global tourist magnet, the island served as a critical tin-mining hub in the 19th century. This industry attracted a diverse wave of immigrants, particularly from China, whose influence is still woven into the island's unique Sino-Portuguese architecture and local traditions.

As the tin reserves eventually dwindled in the 20th century, Phuket pivoted with remarkable success. It transformed from an industrial center into a world-class tourism destination, leveraging its pristine coastline and lush landscapes to become one of Southeast Asia's most beloved island escapes.
